The luthier is one of the so-called 'art professions', a traditional profession, which represents Human excellence in the world. The lute, the instrument from which it takes its name, is one of the oldest bowed instruments. The luthier is a highly specialized craftsman who makes, repairs and restores all stringed musical instruments (violins, cellos, etc.) and plucked (guitars, basses, mandolins, etc.), combining ancient techniques with modern technologies. Today there is also an industrial production of these instruments but the artisan tradition remains very important because it allows you to create customized products, with characteristics and sound quality far superior to those made in series. The luthier takes care of every phase of creation: design, choice of materials, creation of pieces, painting, assembly, finishing and fine-tuning and marketing. The luthier performs the following actions: - He designs the instrument and realizes the drawing. - Choose the wood to use. - Cut the various pieces (soundboard, back, side bands, etc.). - Reinforce the pieces. - Assemble and glue the various parts. - Smooths and finishes the pieces. - Paints them and applies resins. - Mount the tailpiece and fingerboard. - Make the bridge and soul. - Carry out sound tests and adjust the sound of the instrument. The luthier also deals with: - Repair of damaged instruments. - Adjustment of tools and maintenance (such as paint retouching). - Estimates and appraisals of ancient instruments (identifying for example the author, the origin, the value). - Restoration and conservation actions of ancient instruments. It is a work of great precision and patience, because even a small defect can cause distortion in the sound of the instrument and make it unusable. A violin, for example, is made up of about 80 different pieces and it takes up to two months of work to make a finished instrument. The luthier often works alone or with some worker and apprentice employed by him. Its activity takes place mainly indoors, in a small artisan workshop, with considerable organizational and schedule independence. If the laboratory is also connected to the sale to the public, the luthier must also create a showcase with some ready-made instruments and sell objects and accessories: cases, books, spare parts of the strings, etc.
